## Overview

The Albania Highlands landrace is an indica-leaning cannabis population native to the rugged mountainous interior of Albania — a country of the western Balkans bordered by Montenegro, Kosovo, North Macedonia, and Greece. The terrain here is among the most challenging in Europe, dominated by steep limestone ranges, narrow river gorges, and high-altitude plateaus. This population is associated with the isolated communities of the Albanian highlands, where difficult terrain historically limited contact with neighboring cannabis gene pools and allowed a relatively distinct local ecotype to persist.

## History

Cannabis cultivation in the Albanian highlands is commonly reported as extending back many generations, though formal documentation is sparse. The region's geographic isolation — marked by the Accursed Mountains (Bjeshkët e Namuna) and the Dinaric Alps — likely helped preserve older landrace characteristics by limiting genetic exchange with lowland and coastal populations. Grower accounts suggest the plant has long been used for both fiber and resin in rural highland communities, persisting as a semi-feral or cultivated population in terraced fields and village gardens.

## Description

This population is described as an indica-leaning highland landrace of medium height, consistent with many Balkan mountain ecotypes adapted to shorter growing seasons and high-UV conditions at elevation. Plants are commonly reported to develop compact, resinous structure suited to cold nights and dry alpine summers. Leaf morphology is typically broader than Mediterranean sativa types, reflecting the indica-leaning character of high-altitude Balkan populations. Flowering is reported in the range of 8–12 weeks.

- **Height:** Medium
- **Flowering:** 8–12 weeks
- **Type:** Indica-leaning highland landrace
- **Climate:** Mountain Mediterranean

## Grow Information

Adapted to limestone soils, dry summers, and cold mountain winters, this population is considered cold-tolerant relative to lowland Balkan strains. Growers working with similar highland Balkan genetics note good resistance to temperature swings and drought stress. The mountain Mediterranean climate of Albania means hot, dry summers at lower elevations giving way to cool, moist conditions at higher altitudes — plants from this region are commonly reported to handle both extremes reasonably well. Indoor growers may find the 8–12 week flowering window manageable, while outdoor cultivation in temperate climates should allow adequate finishing time.

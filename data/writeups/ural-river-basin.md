## Overview

The Ural River Basin population grows along and around the Ural River watershed in Russia, a geographic corridor that forms one of the traditional boundaries between Europe and Asia. This intermediate (Indica–Sativa) feral population occupies a continental grassland environment where steppe transitions to riparian vegetation. The Ural River basin is considered an important transition zone, and the cannabis populations found there reflect that geographic complexity, showing considerable morphological variability. The Ural River corridor crosses the Russia–Kazakhstan frontier, and these feral populations extend into northwestern Kazakhstan.

## History

The Ural River corridor has long served as a route for both human movement and botanical dispersal between Central Asia and Eastern Europe. Cannabis is thought to have traveled and naturalized through this kind of steppe river system over many centuries, likely assisted by nomadic and trading peoples who used hemp for fiber and other purposes. The resulting feral populations in the Ural River watershed are commonly described as naturalized rather than actively cultivated, with genetics that may reflect a blend of influences from both European hemp traditions and Central Asian landrace populations.

## Description
- **Origin:** Russia (Europe)
- **Climate:** Steppe / Semi-arid (recorded as “Continental grassland”)
- **Morphotype:** Intermediate (NLD–BLD)
- **Chemotype:** Type I (inferred)
- **Domestication:** Feral (escaped)
- **Vernacular type:** Feral
- **Height:** Medium — grower accounts suggest moderately sized plants suited to open grassland conditions
- **Flowering Time:** 10–14 weeks; a longer season consistent with the latitude and the continental grassland climate of the Ural River watershed

Intermediate morphology, reflecting the transitional genetics of the region; neither strongly Indica nor strongly Sativa in appearance. Populations in this corridor are typically described as highly variable in appearance, a hallmark of naturalized feral cannabis with mixed heritage.

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Continental grassland — plants are associated with warm summers and cold winters, with lower humidity than coastal environments
- **Adaptation:** Reported to be robust and well adapted to open, exposed growing conditions typical of steppe terrain along river systems
- **Outdoor season:** The 10–14 week flowering time fits a mid-latitude continental summer, though growers at the higher end of this range should account for early frosts common in the region
- **Yield:** As a feral population, yield expectations are modest and variable; selections may exist with more consistent expression
- **Genetics:** The intermediate character of this population makes it potentially interesting for breeding projects exploring the Indica–Sativa spectrum within a cold-hardy background

## Overview

The Bosnia Highlands landrace is an intermediate cannabis population from the Dinaric Alps of Bosnia and Herzegovina — a mountainous country in the western Balkans sharing borders with Croatia, Serbia, and Montenegro. The Dinaric Alps dominate Bosnia's landscape, characterized by high karst limestone plateaus, dense coniferous forests, and river canyons carved by the Neretva, Bosna, and Drina rivers. Cold winters, humid summers, and significant snowfall define the highland climate here. This population is understood as potentially preserving older Balkan hemp and cannabis genetics shaped by these demanding mountain conditions.

## History

Bosnia and Herzegovina has a documented tradition of cannabis cultivation extending through the Ottoman period and into the twentieth century, when hemp production was practiced across parts of the country. The highland populations of the Dinaric Alps likely represent a mix of cultivated hemp escape and older landrace persistence in rural areas that experienced relatively limited outside influence during periods of political isolation. The potential preservation of older Balkan genetics is noted in the summary, reflecting the region's geographic insularity and long agricultural continuity.

## Description

As a medium-height intermediate landrace with a 9–13 week flowering window, Bosnia Highlands cannabis is adapted to the continental mountain climate of the Dinaric Alps — tolerating cold winters, humid summers, and the considerable altitude and karst terrain of inland Bosnia. The intermediate (indica–sativa) character reflects the mixed Balkan heritage of the population, occupying a phenotypic space between the compact highland indica types of the eastern Balkans and the more sativa-influenced Mediterranean populations of the Adriatic coast.

- **Height:** Medium
- **Flowering:** 9–13 weeks
- **Type:** Mountain landrace, Intermediate (Indica–Sativa)
- **Climate:** Continental mountain

## Grow Information

Adaptation to the cold, humid mountain conditions of the Dinaric Alps in Bosnia and Herzegovina suggests above-average cold tolerance and resistance to moisture-related stress. Growers note that Balkan highland intermediates generally perform well in temperate European climates, finishing in the 9–13 week window before autumn frosts. The limestone karst soils of Bosnia may have selected for some tolerance of alkaline growing conditions. Phenotypic diversity should be expected in an unselected highland population of this type.

## Overview

Qinghai Highland Hemp is a high-altitude hemp population associated with Qinghai Province in western China — a vast plateau province encompassing the eastern portion of the Tibetan Plateau, at elevations typically exceeding 3,000 metres above sea level. Qinghai's plateau cold climate imposes severe constraints on agriculture, and hemp cultivated here has been shaped by conditions of intense UV radiation, low temperatures, short growing seasons, and thin air. This ancient Chinese hemp population is adapted to one of the most challenging agricultural environments in the world.

## History

Hemp cultivation on the Tibetan Plateau and its eastern margins, including Qinghai, is part of China's deep agricultural hemp tradition. The high altitude and cold climate of Qinghai would have necessitated selection for early finishing and cold tolerance in any cultivated population. Whether the Qinghai Highland population represents a continuously maintained traditional landrace or a population descended from earlier cultivation that has now partly naturalized is not well documented. It is classified as an ancient Chinese hemp population, consistent with a long history of cultivation in the region for fiber and seed under plateau conditions.

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Plateau cold; characterized by very high UV intensity, low temperatures even in summer, thin air, and short frost-free growing windows
- **Altitude adaptation:** Hemp at Qinghai altitudes must cope with frost risk throughout the growing season and cannot rely on the warmth available to lowland Chinese hemp varieties
- **Season:** 10–14 week flowering fits within the limited warm season at Qinghai elevations, typically June through September
- **Grow notes:** Qinghai Highland Hemp is of interest for its plateau altitude adaptation, which has selected for UV tolerance and cold hardiness not common in hemp lineages from lower elevations. These traits may have value for breeding programs targeting high-altitude or cold-climate hemp cultivation.

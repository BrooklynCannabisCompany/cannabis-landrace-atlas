## Overview

The Sangha Basin entry describes a feral rainforest cannabis population associated with the Sangha River Basin — a vast tributary system of the Congo River spanning parts of Cameroon, the Central African Republic, and the Republic of Congo. This very tall, very-long-flowering feral population occupies one of the most remote and biodiverse tropical rainforest zones in Africa. It is described as one of the continent's least-studied cannabis regions, and is noted for exceptional humidity tolerance and a highly diverse feral gene pool.

## History

The Sangha Basin is part of the larger Congo Basin rainforest — the second-largest tropical rainforest on Earth — and is among the most sparsely populated and least accessible regions of central Africa. Formal documentation of cannabis in the Sangha River area is essentially absent from published botanical or ethnobotanical literature. Any cannabis growing in this region is almost certainly feral, reproducing through naturalized seed dispersal in the rainforest understory or at forest edges. The gene pool in such an environment would be shaped entirely by ecological selection rather than human breeding, making it potentially a unique reservoir of equatorial rainforest-adapted genetics.

## Description
- **Origin:** Congo (Africa)
- **Climate:** Tropical Rainforest (recorded as “Congo rainforest”)
- **Morphotype:** Unclassified
- **Chemotype:** Type I (inferred)
- **Domestication:** Feral (escaped)
- **Vernacular type:** Feral
- **Height:** Very tall — adapted to compete for light in a closed-canopy rainforest, with elongated growth reaching toward canopy gaps
- **Flowering Time:** 12–18 weeks — long flowering window typical of equatorial feral populations where seasonal photoperiod changes are minimal

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Congo rainforest — extreme and near-constant humidity, very high rainfall (among the highest in Africa), warm equatorial temperatures, and dense canopy shade
- **Adaptation:** Presumed to have exceptional humidity tolerance and resistance to the intense mold pressure of the equatorial rainforest environment; adaptations for low-light and waterlogged soil conditions are also likely
- **Outdoor suitability:** Theoretically suited to wet tropical environments; this genetic material, if ever accessed, could be valuable for breeding extreme humidity and mold resistance
- **Flowering time:** 12–18 weeks; reflecting equatorial conditions with minimal seasonal light variation
- **Notes:** The Sangha Basin represents one of the most significant gaps in African landrace documentation; accessing and preserving genetic material from this region faces extreme logistical challenges; any collection from this area would be of high conservation value

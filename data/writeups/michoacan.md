## Overview

Michoacán is a sativa landrace from the state of Michoacán in west-central Mexico, situated where the Sierra Madre del Sur meets the Trans-Mexican Volcanic Belt. The state's diverse topography includes high pine-forested mountains, deep valleys, and Pacific coastal lowlands, but the landrace associated with this name is typically linked to the highland pine-mountain-forest zones where cannabis has historically been cultivated. Michoacán is one of the Mexican states with a recognized cannabis cultivation tradition, and this population reflects adaptation to the cool, resinous conditions of highland Mexican mountain forest.

## History

Michoacán has a long history as an agricultural region, home to the Purépecha (Tarascan) civilization before Spanish colonization. Cannabis cultivation in the state developed during and after the colonial era, with mountain communities in the Sierra regions developing locally adapted populations. Michoacán-origin cannabis developed a reputation for resinous quality, and strains from this state circulated in the North American market during the 1960s–1980s. The specific genetics of any population labeled "Michoacán" vary, as the name became associated with a regional style as much as a single discrete population. The pine-forest highland context suggests selection for resin production and cold-night tolerance.

## Description

Michoacán is commonly described as a resinous Mexican highland sativa, reflecting adaptation to the cool pine-forest mountain environment. Plants are reported as tall-growing with the characteristic structure of high-altitude Mexican sativas.

- **Height:** Tall
- **Flowering:** 11–14 weeks
- **Climate adaptation:** Pine mountain forest; cool nights, dry summers, high altitude
- **Category:** Sativa
- **Notable trait:** Reported resinous quality associated with highland adaptation

## Grow Information

Growers working with Michoacán-origin genetics can expect tall, resinous sativa plants with reasonable flowering times compared to some other Mexican landraces. The pine mountain forest origin suggests tolerance for cooler nights and lower humidity than coastal or jungle-adapted strains. Outdoor cultivation in warm, dry-summer highland climates is most suitable. The 11–14 week flowering window is manageable, and the resinous character reportedly associated with this population makes it of interest to breeders. Verified grow reports specific to true Michoacán landrace stock are limited, as the name has been applied loosely in the seed trade.

## Overview

The Heilongjiang cannabis population is a ruderalis-leaning, auto-adapted feral population from Heilongjiang Province in the far northeast of China — the country's northernmost province, bordering Russia along the Amur River. Heilongjiang experiences one of China's coldest and shortest growing seasons, with long winters, deep frosts, and a summer period too brief for conventionally photoperiod-dependent cannabis to reliably complete. The feral population found here has adapted accordingly, exhibiting autoflowering or near-autoflowering characteristics that allow plants to flower based on age rather than day length.

## History

Feral cannabis in Heilongjiang is generally understood to be descended from hemp cultivated historically in the region for fiber and seed. Over generations of naturalization in a harsh northern environment, populations with autoflowering tendencies would have had a strong survival advantage — only plants that could complete their cycle within the short frost-free window would successfully set seed. This process of natural selection is the same mechanism believed to have produced ruderalis-type cannabis across the broader northern Eurasian zone. Systematic documentation of the Heilongjiang population specifically is limited in accessible sources.

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Cold northern continental; very cold winters, short warm summers, early autumn frosts
- **Autoflowering character:** The population is described as auto-adapted, meaning it is capable of flowering without a change in photoperiod — a trait of practical value in breeding programs focused on autoflowering cultivars
- **Season:** Can complete in as few as 6–10 weeks from germination, making it viable even in very short northern summers
- **Grow notes:** Of primary interest as a genetic source of reliable cold hardiness and autoflowering traits. Not typically cultivated for yield or resin; value lies in its extreme environmental adaptation.

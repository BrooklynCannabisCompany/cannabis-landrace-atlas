## Overview

Red Lao is a sativa variant from Laos distinguished by its pigmented phenotype expression — most commonly reported as reddish or purple coloration appearing on stems, petioles, or sometimes the flowers themselves. Laos sits within one of Southeast Asia's most historically significant cannabis corridors, spanning the Mekong River valley and the highland jungles of the interior. This particular variant is understood to be a naturally occurring color-expressed phenotype within Lao landrace populations rather than a separate stable breed, adapted to the country's jungle monsoon climate.

## History

Laos has a long association with cannabis cultivation, particularly in rural and highland communities, and Lao cannabis was notable on the international market during the 1960s–1970s. Within those broad Lao gene pools, pigmented phenotypes are occasionally reported by collectors who encountered reddish stems or anthocyanin expression in the flowers. Such pigmentation in tropical cannabis is generally understood to arise from natural genetic variation rather than cold stress (which triggers anthocyanins in temperate varieties), suggesting this trait is partly heritable within certain Lao populations. Documentation of Red Lao as a consistently isolated population is limited.

## Overview

Meo Thai is a sativa landrace associated with highland tropical growing zones in Thailand, specifically connected to the Hmong (historically called Meo or Meao in Thai) cultivation tradition within the Thai highland context. As a named population it overlaps conceptually with the broader Thai Highland Hmong genetic zone but is catalogued here as a distinct Thai entry, associated with the traditional Thai stick genetics produced by Hmong and other hill tribe communities cultivating cannabis in Thailand's highland zones. This population sits within the traditional Thai landrace complex and carries the character associated with highland Thai cannabis cultivation.

## History

The Meo Thai designation reflects the historical use of "Meo" as the Thai term for the Hmong people — an ethnic group with a strong presence in the highlands of Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai, and adjacent northern provinces. Hmong cannabis cultivation in Thailand was historically associated with both subsistence use and the trade that produced Thai sticks — a preparation of cannabis flowers on bamboo sticks that became internationally recognized during the latter twentieth century. The Meo Thai population represents the cannabis tradition of these highland communities as situated within Thai borders, distinct from the Lao Hmong Highland material by geography and the specific highland conditions of northern Thailand. Documentation of this specific population as a distinct landrace entry is limited primarily to grower and collector accounts.

## Overview

Bhutan Highland refers to cannabis populations originating in the Kingdom of Bhutan, a landlocked Himalayan nation wedged between India and China. Bhutan's terrain is defined by steep river valleys, dense forest, and elevations that range from subtropical foothills to alpine peaks above 7,000 meters. The cannabis populations here are adapted to mountain valley conditions — cool nights, intense high-altitude sunlight, and strong seasonal variation — and are classified as a Himalayan landrace with mixed morphological expression. Bhutan's traditional relationship with cannabis is complex; the plant grows semi-wild and has historically been used as animal fodder more than for psychoactive purposes.

## History

Unlike neighboring Nepal and India's Himalayan states, Bhutan has not historically been a center of hash or charas commerce. Cannabis grows abundantly in Bhutan's mid-altitude zones as a semi-feral plant, and documentation of intentional landrace cultivation is limited. The country's isolation — it opened to outside visitors only in the 1970s — means that its cannabis populations developed with little external genetic influence. Collector accounts note that Bhutanese cannabis can display a broad range of morphological variation, suggesting a genetically diverse population shaped by altitude, valley microclimate, and centuries of semi-feral selection.

## Description

- **Type:** Himalayan landrace (Mixed)
- **Height:** Tall
- **Flowering:** 12–18 weeks — among the longer ranges documented for Himalayan landraces
- **Climate:** Mountain valley
- Mixed morphology: plants range from broader-leafleted, more indica-adjacent expressions in higher, colder valleys to taller, more sativa-leaning forms in warmer lower elevations
- Adapted to intense UV radiation at altitude and to the sharp diurnal temperature swings characteristic of Himalayan mountain valleys
- Resin production is commonly reported as present but variable; phenotypic diversity is a defining trait of this broad population

## Grow Information

- The exceptionally long flowering window (12–18 weeks) distinguishes Bhutan Highland from most landrace cannabis; outdoor cultivation requires a very long, warm season
- Mountain valley adaptation means plants tolerate cool nights better than many tropical landraces but may underperform in consistently humid or warm lowland conditions
- Phenotypic diversity within the population means growers may encounter significant variation plant-to-plant; selection across generations is recommended
- Indoors, the tall stature and very long flowering time make this a challenging but rewarding project for dedicated landrace cultivators
- Best suited to high-altitude or continental climates with long summers and dry autumns

## Overview

Gansu Hemp is an ancient fiber and seed hemp selection associated with Gansu Province in northwestern China — a long, geographically diverse corridor province that spans from the edge of the Tibetan Plateau in the south to the Gobi Desert and the Hexi Corridor in the north. Gansu sits at a historical crossroads: it was a critical passage along the Silk Road, and cannabis has been cultivated in the region for fiber and grain for thousands of years. The Gansu Hemp population reflects this deep agricultural heritage rather than selection for resin or drug use.

## History

China is widely regarded as one of the earliest sites of cannabis cultivation, with documented use of hemp fiber and seed dating back thousands of years in ancient Chinese records. Gansu, as part of the northwestern agricultural heartland and a gateway to Central Asia, is commonly associated with early Chinese hemp cultivation traditions. Selection in this region has historically emphasized fiber quality and seed production under steppe conditions. The population is considered an ancient Chinese selection line, though the precise genealogy of surviving Gansu hemp strains is not well documented in accessible sources.

## Description
- **Origin:** China (East Asia / North Asia)
- **Climate:** Steppe / Semi-arid
- **Morphotype:** Narrow-Leaf Hemp
- **Chemotype:** Type V (inferred)
- **Domestication:** Domesticated
- **Vernacular type:** Hemp
- **Height:** Tall, consistent with fiber hemp types bred for long stalk production
- **Flowering Time:** 10–14 weeks under steppe photoperiod conditions

Typically sturdy and upright with minimal branching, as is characteristic of dense-sown fiber hemp cultivation. Likely intermediate to narrow, adapted to the drier, sunnier conditions of northwestern China. Low THC, as expected of a traditional fiber/hemp selection; CBD content undocumented.

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Steppe; dry, sunny, with cold winters and warm summers; moderate annual precipitation
- **Soil:** Adapted to the loessial and alluvial soils characteristic of Gansu's agricultural river valleys
- **Season:** 10–14 week flowering fits within the warm season available along Gansu's main river valleys
- **Grow notes:** As a traditional fiber hemp, Gansu Hemp is best suited to open field conditions with adequate spacing for stalk development. Its ancient selection for steppe conditions suggests good drought tolerance relative to more tropical hemp types.

## Overview

Kalinga is a highland sativa landrace from Kalinga Province in the Cordillera Administrative Region of Luzon, Philippines. Situated in the rugged mountain terrain of the northern Cordillera highlands at significant elevation, Kalinga experiences a climate characterized by heavy rainfall, high humidity, and cooler temperatures than the Philippine lowlands. The province is home to the Kalinga people, known for their warrior traditions and rich indigenous culture. Cannabis from this region is commonly associated with strong tropical adaptation, excellent vigor, and a close genetic relationship to the neighboring Ifugao and Benguet populations of the Luzon Cordillera.

## History

The Kalinga highlands have been home to indigenous Kalinga communities whose agricultural practices have shaped the highland landscape over many centuries. Traditional cultivation of diverse crops — including rice, root vegetables, and various medicinal and useful plants — has characterized Kalinga upland farming. Cannabis cultivation in the Kalinga highlands is part of the broader Cordillera highland landrace tradition; the specific Kalinga population likely shares common ancestry with Benguet and Ifugao cannabis while having developed its own locally adapted characteristics through generations of cultivation in the distinctive microenvironments of Kalinga's valleys and ridges. Formal documentation of Kalinga cannabis specifically is limited; most available information comes from regional ethnobotanical accounts and grower observations.

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Cordillera highlands; heavy rainfall, high humidity, elevated and cooler than Philippine lowlands, with pronounced seasonal monsoon
- **Flowering time:** 12–18 weeks; long-season highland variety
- **Vigor:** Commonly reported as excellent — consistent with other Luzon Cordillera highland cannabis populations
- **Related populations:** Likely closely related to Benguet and Ifugao; growers familiar with those varieties will find similar cultivation requirements
- **Cultivation notes:** A valuable component of the Luzon Cordillera landrace complex; best suited to outdoor highland or greenhouse cultivation; of interest for both horticultural use and genetic preservation

## Overview

Tahiti is a feral sativa population associated with the island of Tahiti in French Polynesia, the principal island of the Society Islands group in the central South Pacific. Tahiti's coastal environment is characterised by a warm, humid tropical climate with trade wind influence, high year-round temperatures, and significant salt-laden air exposure — conditions that have shaped this population's noted tolerance for salt and humidity. Sitting at roughly 17–18° south latitude, Tahiti experiences moderate seasonal daylength variation, which is reflected in the population's relatively compact 10–13 week flowering window for a tropical type.

## History

Cannabis is not indigenous to French Polynesia; its presence on Tahiti is attributed to historical introduction, with colonial-era contact, trade routes, and subsequent human movement providing the most plausible pathways. Once established, plants naturalised in coastal and inland areas of the island, gradually adapting to Tahiti's specific environmental conditions without formal cultivation management. The relatively isolated position of French Polynesia in the Pacific means this feral population developed with limited genetic input from outside the island group over time. Specific introduction dates or sources are not documented in available records.

## Description

Tahiti plants are classified as a feral sativa with a tall growth habit, consistent with sativa-lineage populations in warm coastal tropical environments. The 10–13 week flowering window is notably shorter than many other Pacific feral sativas, possibly reflecting adaptation to Tahiti's mild seasonal cues and the practical selection pressure of coastal exposure. Salt tolerance and humidity tolerance are the population's most cited adaptive characteristics — both essential traits for plants persisting in a coastal island environment. Open bud architecture suited to humid, wind-exposed conditions is expected.

- **Height:** Tall
- **Flowering:** 10–13 weeks
- **Climate preference:** Coastal island (tropical)
- **Category:** Feral

## Grow Information

Tahiti's coastal island origin gives this feral population a distinctive ecological profile: tolerance for salt air, humidity, and the variable wind and rain typical of Pacific island conditions. Outdoor cultivation in tropical or warm subtropical coastal environments is most appropriate. The relatively shorter flowering period (10–13 weeks) for a tropical sativa may be an advantage for growers in locations with a defined season. Indoor cultivation is feasible given its tall-but-not-extreme stature. As with all feral populations, phenotypic variability should be expected. No verified yield or cannabinoid data is available.

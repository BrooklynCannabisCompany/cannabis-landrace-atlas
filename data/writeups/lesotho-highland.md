## Overview

Lesotho Highland is a medium-tall sativa landrace from the mountain kingdom of Lesotho — a landlocked country entirely surrounded by South Africa and positioned almost entirely above 1,400 metres elevation. Lesotho's cold highland climate, with its harsh winters, short growing season, and strong summer sunlight, has produced a cannabis population notable for its cold adaptation — rare among African landraces — and a citrus-resin terpene profile. Lesotho has a history of cannabis production that has made it a significant supplier within southern Africa.

## History

Lesotho has been one of southern Africa's more significant cannabis-producing territories, with cultivation traditionally concentrated in the highland valleys and slopes. The Basotho people have historically cultivated cannabis as a crop with cultural and economic significance, and the harsh mountain conditions have over generations shaped an ecotype with notable resilience to cold and altitude. Lesotho cannabis has been recognized within southern African trade networks and has attracted some attention from international landrace collectors, though formal documentation of the highland ecotype as a distinct variety is limited.

## Grow Information

The Lesotho Highland population is among the more cold-tolerant of African sativas, a direct result of its high-altitude homeland. This makes it potentially better suited to cooler temperate outdoor climates than most African landraces. The moderate stature and 10–13 week flowering window make it a more practical outdoor option for growers in cool climates. Well-draining soils and strong direct sunlight, as found in its mountain habitat, will support best performance. Growers in cold-climate regions may find this one of the more accessible African landrace options.

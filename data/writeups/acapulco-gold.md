## Overview
Acapulco Gold is a celebrated sativa landrace associated with the Guerrero region
around Acapulco on Mexico's Pacific coast. It is one of the most famous traditional
Mexican cannabis populations, known historically for golden-hued, resinous flowers
and an energetic effect.

## History
The name became widely known in North America during the 1960s–70s, when Mexican
sativas were a major part of the imported cannabis trade. Grower accounts commonly
describe a tropical-highland population from the mountains behind Acapulco; as with
most landraces, specific lineage and selection history are not rigorously documented
and modern seed lines sold under the name vary in authenticity.

## Description
- Tall, lanky sativa structure with long internodes.
- Flowers commonly reported to show golden/amber tones; aromas described as sweet,
  toffee-like, citrus, and woody.
- Effects typically reported as uplifting and energetic.

## Grow Information
Adapted to a warm tropical-highland climate with a long season. Commonly reported as
tall with an extended flowering time (roughly 12–16 weeks), favoring growers with
warm climates or ample indoor height. Vigorous and stretch-prone; better suited to
long-season outdoor cultivation than short-summer climates.

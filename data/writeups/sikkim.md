## Overview

Sikkim is a sativa landrace from the northeastern Indian state of Sikkim, a small, mountainous region bordered by Nepal, Tibet, and Bhutan. Sikkim's climate is dominated by humid mountain jungle environments at lower elevations transitioning to high-altitude temperate forest, with significant monsoon rainfall and exceptional biodiversity. Cannabis in Sikkim grows both wild and semi-cultivated, and populations in the region have been noted for strong vigor and adaptation to the dense forest understory and margins at varying elevations.

## History

Sikkim has a complex cultural history influenced by Lepcha, Bhutia, and Nepali communities, all of whom have historically inhabited the region and maintained relationships with local plant life including cannabis. The state is positioned along a botanical corridor of extraordinary richness — the eastern Himalayas — and cannabis here has coexisted with one of the world's most biodiverse forest environments. Formal documentation of distinct Sikkimese cannabis populations is limited; most references group northeastern Indian landraces broadly. Grower accounts suggest that plants from this region are notably vigorous, likely reflecting adaptation to the humid, competitive forest ecology.

## Overview

Montagnard is a sativa landrace associated with the indigenous highland peoples of Vietnam's Central Highlands — a region the French colonial administration collectively termed "Montagnard" (mountain people). This designation encompasses diverse ethnic groups including the Jarai, Bahnar, Ede, Mnong, and others who inhabit the highland plateau and forested mountain zones of Vietnam's interior. Growing under a highland jungle climate with pronounced monsoon rainfall and moderately cooler temperatures than the Vietnamese coastal lowlands, this population represents the tribal forest lineage of Vietnamese highland cannabis.

## History

The indigenous Montagnard peoples have maintained distinct agricultural and cultural traditions in the Central Highlands of Vietnam — spanning the modern provinces of Kon Tum, Gia Lai, Đắk Lắk, Đắk Nông, and Lâm Đồng — for many centuries. Cannabis cultivation within Montagnard communities is documented in regional ethnographic literature as part of traditional plant use, though the specific characteristics of individual community-associated populations are rarely described in detail. The Central Highlands represent a geographic and cultural corridor between the lowland Vietnamese coast and the Cambodian and Lao highland zones, suggesting that the Montagnard cannabis population may carry genetic influences from across this broader Indochina highland belt. This is a distinct population from the better-documented Dalat highland type, reflecting different ethnic and ecological contexts within the same broader region.

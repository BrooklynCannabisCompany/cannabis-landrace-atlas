## Overview

Northern Territory Bush Cannabis refers to a remote feral cannabis population found in Australia's Northern Territory — a vast, sparsely populated jurisdiction covering the top third of the Australian continent, characterized by tropical savanna in the north and arid desert in the south. This feral population is described as having mixed ancestry, suggesting introduction from multiple source populations rather than descent from a single coherent landrace. The tropical savanna climate of the Top End — with its pronounced wet and dry seasons, intense heat, and seasonal monsoon rainfall — defines the primary environmental context for this population.

## History

The history of cannabis naturalization in Australia's Northern Territory is not formally documented. The Northern Territory's Top End has historically experienced contact with Macassan fishermen from Sulawesi over centuries, and later colonial contact introduced diverse populations to the region. Any of these contact pathways could theoretically have introduced cannabis to the region at various points, though no verified historical records confirm this for the Northern Territory specifically. The mixed ancestry description suggests that the current feral population may be the product of multiple introduction events or genetic mixing from different source populations rather than a single founding event.

## Description
- **Origin:** Australia (Oceania)
- **Climate:** Tropical savanna — pronounced wet and dry seasons in the Top End, intense heat, monsoonal summer rainfall, long dry winter.
- **Morphotype:** Narrow-Leaf Drug
- **Chemotype:** Type I (inferred)
- **Domestication:** Feral (escaped)
- **Vernacular type:** Feral
- **Height:** Tall; consistent with sativa-type feral cannabis in a tropical savanna environment.
- **Flowering Time:** Variable length; reflecting the mixed ancestry and feral, unselected character of this population.

Remote feral population with mixed ancestry — significant genetic variability expected.

## Grow Information

Northern Territory Bush Cannabis, as a mixed-ancestry tropical savanna feral population, is likely adapted to the extreme heat and seasonal drought of the Top End's dry season, as well as the intense monsoon rains of the wet season. Variable flowering length reflects the lack of selection uniformity in a feral population. Heat tolerance is a probable adaptive trait given the Territory's climate extremes. Very high phenotypic variability is expected given the mixed ancestry description. This population is of potential interest for heat and drought tolerance traits, though cultivation documentation is essentially absent.

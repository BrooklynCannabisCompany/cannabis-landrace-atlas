## Overview

The Romanian Hemp Landrace is a traditional hemp population from Romania — a large Central/Eastern European country occupying the lower Danube basin, Carpathian arc, and Pannonian plain, bordered by Ukraine, Moldova, Bulgaria, Serbia, and Hungary. Romania has one of Europe's most significant and historically deep hemp cultivation traditions, with fiber hemp grown across the Carpathian foothills, Moldavian plateau, and Wallachian plains for centuries. The population is described as very tall, vigorous, and among the most important historic hemp gene pools in Europe for its strong fiber traits and overall plant vigor.

## History

Romanian hemp cultivation is documented from at least the medieval period and intensified substantially during the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries, when Romania was an important supplier of hemp fiber and seed to European markets. The variety of ecological zones within Romania — from the Carpathian highlands to the Danube floodplain — allowed different locally adapted hemp ecotypes to develop across the country. Romanian hemp varieties were incorporated into Soviet-era breeding programs and are recognized in European hemp genetics as important contributors to modern fiber varieties. The strong fiber traits and vigor noted in the summary reflect centuries of selection for these qualities in the demanding continental lowland climate of Romania.

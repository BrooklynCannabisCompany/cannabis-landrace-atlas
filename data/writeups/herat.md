## Overview

The Herat landrace is a traditional hash indica from the Herat region of western Afghanistan — a historic city and agricultural province situated at the intersection of the Iranian Plateau, the Afghan highlands, and the Central Asian steppe. Herat is one of the great cities of the Islamic world, a former capital of the Timurid Empire and a longstanding center of Persian cultural influence in Afghanistan. Its cannabis is associated with the western Afghan cannabis tradition, shaped by drought tolerance, resin production, and adaptation to the desert-edge environment of the Hari River valley and its surrounding arid uplands.

## History

Herat's position on trade routes connecting Persia, Central Asia, and the Indian subcontinent made it a center of cultural and commercial exchange for centuries. The influence of Persian trade and cultural norms on Herat — stronger here than in much of Afghanistan — is commonly thought to have shaped the cannabis cultivation tradition of the region, with connections to both Iranian cannabis lineages to the west and the core Afghan landrace populations of the Hindu Kush and Helmand regions to the east and south. Grower accounts suggest that Herat cannabis occupies a transitional genetic position between Iranian plateau and Afghan mountain cannabis types. Specific documented history of this distinct population in cannabis literature is limited, but the regional context supports a long tradition of cannabis cultivation for hashish production.

## Description

- **Category:** Indica
- **Height:** Medium
- **Stature:** Moderate; adapted to open cultivation in a valley-and-plateau agricultural landscape
- **Flowering:** 8–11 weeks; moderate for an Afghan/Iranian-border indica type
- **Resin:** Good; drought tolerance and resin production are the commonly cited adaptive traits for western Afghan populations
- The desert-edge environment of Herat — hot, dry, and sunny — shapes a resilient plant with strong sun and drought adaptation.

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Arid plateau — hot dry summers, cold winters, very low annual rainfall (roughly 230–300 mm); the Hari River valley provides irrigation access for agriculture
- **Altitude:** Herat city sits at approximately 900 m; surrounding uplands are higher
- **Drought tolerance:** High; a defining trait shaped by the western Afghan arid climate
- **Heat tolerance:** High; Herat summers are hot, with July temperatures frequently exceeding 35°C
- **Season:** The 8–11 week flowering window allows harvest before the onset of cold autumn nights
- Cultivators should expect a resilient, heat- and drought-tolerant indica type with moderate-to-good resin production.

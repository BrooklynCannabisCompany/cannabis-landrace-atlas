## Overview

Chitral is a classic indica landrace from Chitral District in the northernmost part of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, Pakistan — a high, narrow valley system carved by the Chitral River (Kunar) deep within the Hindu Kush mountain range. Chitral is commonly regarded as one of the most important traditional charas-producing regions in Pakistan, and its cannabis is closely tied to the Hindu Kush gene pool. The population is characterized by fast flowering, cold hardiness, short compact stature, and heavy resin production shaped by altitude, cold stress, and generations of human selection for charas quality.

## History

Chitral's cannabis tradition is rooted in the region's long history of isolation behind the Hindu Kush. The district's distinct Kho culture and its geographic separation from lowland Pakistan contributed to the preservation of traditional cultivation practices. Charas — hand-rubbed cannabis resin — is the primary traditional cannabis product of the Chitral region, and cultivar selection has historically favored maximum resin yield from compact, cold-hardy plants suited to the short alpine growing season. Chitral cannabis is frequently cited in the landrace literature as a representative Hindu Kush indica type, and seeds from this region have entered Western cannabis breeding collections since at least the 1970s and 1980s.

## Description

- **Category:** Indica
- **Height:** Short — compact, dense bush morphology
- **Stature:** Heavy branching with broad, dark-green indica leaves; tight internode spacing
- **Flowering:** 7–9 weeks; fast-finishing, as required by the brief alpine growing season
- **Resin:** Very high; resin production is the primary trait for which this population has been traditionally selected, consistent with charas production practices
- The Chitral phenotype is typically cited as an archetype of the compact, resin-heavy Hindu Kush indica.

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Hindu Kush alpine valley — cold nights year-round at elevation, hot days in summer, very short frost-free growing window; dry climate with some winter snowfall
- **Altitude:** Chitral District ranges from roughly 1,100 m (district center) to over 7,700 m (Tirich Mir); cultivation concentrated in the valley floors and lower mountain slopes
- **Cold tolerance:** High; essential for survival in the Hindu Kush
- **Season:** The 7–9 week flowering window allows completion before the early autumn cold of the high valleys
- Cultivators outside the Hindu Kush should expect a short, manageable, fast-finishing plant with strong resin production — one of the classic compact indicas.

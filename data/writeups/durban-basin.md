## Overview
The Durban basin population is a pure-sativa landrace from the subtropical coastal
region around Durban in KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa. South African "Durban" sativas
are widely associated with early flowering for a sativa, an anise/licorice aroma, and
an energetic daytime effect.

## History
Durban-area landraces are among the better-known African sativas in Western breeding,
commonly reported to have been collected from the KwaZulu-Natal coast and selected by
local growers over generations. They are frequently cited as a source of early-finishing
sativa traits and of elevated THCV in some lines. Specific seed-line provenance varies
and is not formally documented.

## Description
- Tall sativa structure adapted to a subtropical coastal climate.
- Aroma commonly described as licorice/anise with sweet notes.
- Reported to be energetic and clear; some lines are noted for higher THCV.

## Grow Information
Adapted to a warm, subtropical coastal environment. Commonly reported as tall with a
relatively short flowering window for a sativa (roughly 9–11 weeks), which makes
Durban-type genetics popular for outdoor growers in temperate climates where season
length is limited. Generally vigorous; coastal origin suggests reasonable humidity
tolerance, though dense colas should still be watched for mold in very wet conditions.

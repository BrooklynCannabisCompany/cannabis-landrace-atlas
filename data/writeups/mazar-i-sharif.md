## Overview
Mazar I Sharif is a short, fast-finishing indica landrace from the northern Afghan city and region of Mazar-i-Sharif in Balkh Province — an area situated between the Hindu Kush foothills and the Amu Darya river plain bordering Uzbekistan and Turkmenistan. This strain is associated with high resin production and is one of the most recognized Afghan indica names in both hashish tradition and Western cannabis breeding.

## History
Mazar-i-Sharif and the surrounding Balkh region have been a major cannabis and hashish-producing area of northern Afghanistan for generations. The flat, semi-arid terrain and long, hot summers of the Amu Darya lowlands, combined with the proximity of the Hindu Kush highlands, created conditions favoring compact, heavily resinous plants well suited to traditional hashish production. Grower accounts describe Mazar-i-Sharif genetics as among the most potent and resin-dense of the classic Afghan landraces. Seed lines carrying this name entered Western collections and breeding programs in the 1970s–1990s and have since been widely commercialized, though the relationship between commercial "Mazar" lines and original landrace material varies.

## Description
- Short, compact indica with dense, resin-heavy flowers.
- Broad-leaf morphology with thick stems and sturdy branching.
- Exceptionally heavy resin production is the defining characteristic.
- Aromas commonly described as earthy, pungent, and deeply hashy.
- Effects are typically reported as strongly sedating and physically heavy.

## Grow Information
Adapted to the northern Afghan climate — hot, dry summers with cool nights and cold winters. Flowering is fast at 7–9 weeks, making it practical for outdoor cultivation in shorter seasons. Short stature and rapid maturation make it well-suited to indoor growing as well. High resin output is consistent across reported cultivation accounts. Dense flowers are susceptible to botrytis in humid climates; low-humidity growing conditions are strongly preferred.

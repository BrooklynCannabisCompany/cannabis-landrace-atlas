## Overview

The Georgia Highlands landrace is an intermediate cannabis population from the Greater and Lesser Caucasus highlands of Georgia — a country occupying the southern Caucasus between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, bordered by Russia to the north, Turkey and Armenia to the south. The Georgian highlands encompass some of the most rugged terrain in Eurasia, with the Greater Caucasus range forming the northern border and the Lesser Caucasus occupying the south, separated by fertile river valleys including the Mtkvari (Kura) and Rioni corridors. This crossroads position between Europe, Anatolia, Persia, and Central Asia has shaped a historically significant and genetically diverse cannabis population.

## History

Georgia is widely recognized as one of the world's ancient agricultural heartlands, and its Caucasus highland position made it a key corridor for the movement of cannabis genetics between East and West. Archaeological and historical evidence places cannabis use in the Caucasus region in antiquity, and Georgia's role at the intersection of multiple trade and migration routes — including the Silk Road's Caucasus branches — would have introduced diverse cannabis gene pools over millennia. Traditional cannabis cultivation persisted in rural Georgian highland communities through the Soviet period and beyond, with mountain populations maintaining greater genetic integrity than lowland cultivated types.

## Description

As a medium-tall, intermediate (indica–sativa) population with a 9–13 week flowering window adapted to mountain forest conditions of the Caucasus, Georgia Highlands cannabis is described as occupying a balance between the compact, resinous indica character of high-altitude Caucasus types and the more vigorous sativa influence from lower-elevation populations. The mountain forest climate of the Georgian highlands — cool summers, cold snowy winters, and fertile river valleys — selects for robust plants capable of handling both temperature stress and significant moisture.

- **Height:** Medium-tall
- **Flowering:** 9–13 weeks
- **Type:** Mountain landrace, Intermediate (Indica–Sativa)
- **Climate:** Mountain forest

## Grow Information

Adaptation to the rugged Caucasus mountain forest environment of Georgia implies cold tolerance, good vigor on rocky highland soils, and the ability to handle temperature variation between warm valley microclimates and exposed highland ridges. Growers working with similar Caucasus intermediate populations report manageable flowering times and reasonably broad adaptability. The 9–13 week window suits temperate outdoor cultivation, with mountain ecotypes generally finishing before hard autumn frosts in most northern hemisphere climates.

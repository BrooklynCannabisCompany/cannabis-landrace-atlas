## Overview

Rutshuru is a highland equatorial sativa landrace from Rutshuru Territory in North Kivu Province, eastern Democratic Republic of Congo. This region sits within the Albertine Rift — the western branch of the East African Rift system — and is defined by its proximity to active volcanic mountain systems, including the Virunga range. Cannabis grown here is adapted to fertile volcanic soils, high equatorial rainfall, and the complex highland conditions of one of Africa's most geologically and ecologically remarkable zones.

## History

Rutshuru Territory has long been a crossroads of agricultural communities in the eastern Congo highlands, with Bantu and Nilotic-influenced populations historically cultivating the fertile volcanic soils of the North Kivu region. Cannabis cultivation in this area is likely longstanding, though specific documentation of the Rutshuru landrace in botanical or ethnobotanical literature is limited. The Albertine Rift's exceptional biodiversity — often called Africa's "Mountain of the Moon" region — suggests that cannabis populations here may have developed under conditions of relative genetic isolation and intense ecological selection pressure. Grower accounts are sparse for this specific territory.

- **Height:** Tall — consistent with well-nourished equatorial highland sativa growth in the fertile volcanic soils of North Kivu

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Volcanic highland — fertile volcanic soils, high equatorial rainfall, warm days, cooler highland nights, and intense but cloud-moderated sunlight
- **Adaptation:** Likely well adapted to volcanic soil fertility and the high moisture conditions of North Kivu's mountain environment; the Albertine Rift's ecological richness suggests a complex growing environment
- **Outdoor suitability:** Suited to tropical highland environments with good rainfall and fertile, well-draining volcanic or mineral-rich soils
- **Flowering time:** 12–18 weeks outdoors; the long window reflects equatorial highland conditions with limited photoperiod variation
- **Notes:** North Kivu's ongoing political instability has made systematic collection and documentation of Rutshuru cannabis genetics extremely difficult; this population should be considered a conservation priority if material can be responsibly accessed

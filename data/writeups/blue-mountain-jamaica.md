## Overview

Blue Mountain Jamaica is one of the most recognized cannabis landrace populations from the Caribbean, associated with the Blue Mountains of eastern Jamaica. This rugged highland range, which includes some of the highest peaks in the Caribbean, provides a distinctly different growing environment from Jamaica's coastal lowlands — cooler temperatures, persistent cloud and mist, high rainfall, and rich volcanic soils. Cannabis cultivated in and around the Blue Mountains has long been associated with strong vigor, an incense-and-fruit terpene profile, and adaptation to both elevation and the persistent humidity of Jamaica's eastern mountain system.

## History

Jamaica's Blue Mountains have been associated with cannabis cultivation for well over a century, intertwined with the island's cultural, spiritual, and agricultural traditions. The mountain environment selected for populations adapted to cooler temperatures, heavy cloud cover, and the distinctive mineral-rich soils of the Blue Mountain range — the same region famous for its premium coffee. Jamaican cannabis more broadly became internationally influential during the 1960s and 70s, with Blue Mountain populations among the most referenced highland types. Formal genetic documentation of the Blue Mountain population as a distinct landrace is limited, but grower accounts consistently describe it as a distinctive highland ecotype separate from lowland Jamaican cannabis.

## Description
- **Origin:** Jamaica (Americas)
- **Climate:** Tropical mountain — cool and misty, high rainfall, rich volcanic soils, strong diurnal temperature variation
- **Morphotype:** Narrow-Leaf Drug
- **Chemotype:** Type I (inferred)
- **Domestication:** Domesticated
- **Vernacular type:** Sativa
- **Height:** Tall, commonly reaching 2–4 meters in optimal outdoor conditions
- **Flowering Time:** 12–18 weeks; extended flowering typical of highland tropical sativas adapted to the Blue Mountains' cooler temperatures and persistent cloud cover

Typically tall and vigorous with a classic sativa open-branching structure. Grower accounts frequently cite incense, tropical fruit, and spice terpene notes. Adaptation to persistent cloud and mist may contribute to resilience in high-humidity conditions. Strong vigor is a commonly reported trait.

## Grow Information

- Adapted to highland tropical conditions — performs well where cooler nights and humidity are present
- Long flowering period (12–18 weeks) demands a generous outdoor season or controlled-environment management
- Tall growth habit benefits from early training and ample vertical space
- Highland photoperiod adaptation at Jamaican latitudes; flowering induction timing should account for the Blue Mountains' specific seasonal light patterns
- Volcanic mountain soils are typically rich, well-drained, and slightly acidic; replicate with organic, well-aerated growing media

## Overview

Chimbu Province (also known as Simbu Province) is a highland province of Papua New Guinea situated in the central cordillera of the island's mainland, at elevations typically ranging from around 1,500 to over 3,000 metres. The cannabis population associated with Chimbu Province is a highland tropical landrace sativa, shaped by the cool nights, intense equatorial sunlight, and fertile volcanic soils characteristic of the PNG highlands. It is reported to express strong psychoactive character, consistent with the broader category of PNG highland sativas that have attracted attention from collectors and breeders.

## History

Cannabis cultivation in the Papua New Guinea highlands has a long but poorly documented history. In Chimbu Province, as in neighboring highland provinces, small-scale cultivation by local communities appears to have a multi-generational presence, though precise records are lacking. The highland isolation of communities in Chimbu — the rugged terrain historically limited outside contact — is commonly cited as a factor in the development of locally adapted cannabis populations. The province's dense population relative to its highland terrain has long been noted by researchers, and cannabis cultivation has reportedly been integrated into informal local economies.

## Overview

Morobe Province occupies a distinctive position in Papua New Guinea's geography, encompassing both a significant section of the northern coast of the mainland and the rugged Huon Peninsula, with elevations ranging from sea level to over 4,000 metres in the Saruwaged Range. This coastal-to-mountain gradient makes Morobe Province a highland-to-coast genetic transition zone for cannabis — the province's cannabis populations potentially reflect a cline of adaptation across elevation and climate zones. The Morobe landrace is classified as a sativa with a coastal mountain tropical climate, indicating influence from both the humid coastal lowlands and the cooler highland zones.

## History

Cannabis history in Morobe Province is not formally documented. Lae, the provincial capital, is Papua New Guinea's second-largest city and a major port, which has historically made Morobe one of the more connected PNG provinces to outside trade and movement. However, traditional cannabis populations in Morobe's highland and remote coastal areas would have developed through local cultivation practices less influenced by international variety introductions. The province's status as a transition zone between coastal and highland PNG environments suggests the local cannabis gene pool may reflect this ecological diversity.

## Grow Information

Morobe Province cannabis, as a coastal mountain tropical sativa, occupies an intermediate climate niche between the lowland equatorial sativas of PNG's coast and the cooler highland landraces of the interior. The 11–15 week flowering window is more moderate than the pure highland PNG types. Tall growth is expected. Cultivators may find that this population performs well in intermediate conditions — warm but not extreme heat, with some humidity tolerance. As a transition-zone population, phenotypic variability across the elevation cline is likely high, making it potentially interesting for selection work.

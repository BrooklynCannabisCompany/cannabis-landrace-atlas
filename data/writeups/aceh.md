## Overview

Aceh is a sativa landrace originating from the Aceh province at the northern tip of Sumatra, Indonesia — one of the most biologically diverse corners of Southeast Asia. Growing under a tropical rainforest climate characterized by heavy annual rainfall, high humidity, and equatorial heat, this population has adapted to conditions that would challenge most cannabis varieties. Aceh cannabis is commonly associated with strong psychoactive expression and vigorous jungle growth, making it one of the more recognized Indonesian landraces among collectors and ethnobotanists.

## History

Cannabis cultivation in Aceh has deep roots, with the plant historically used within the region's cultural, culinary, and traditional medicinal contexts long before modern prohibition. Aceh's relative geographic isolation — bounded by ocean on three sides and dense highland jungle inland — likely contributed to the development of a phenotypically consistent local population. Grower accounts and collector reports suggest the Aceh landrace has been maintained by local farming communities over many generations, though rigorous documentation of its cultivation lineage is scarce. The region's strongly Islamic cultural identity in the modern era has made ongoing cultivation increasingly clandestine.

## Description

- **Category:** Sativa
- **Type:** Sativa landrace
- **Height:** Very tall — commonly reported to exceed 3 m under natural conditions
- **Flowering:** Long, typically 11–15 weeks from photoperiod induction
- **Morphology:** Grower accounts suggest narrow, elongated leaflets typical of equatorial sativa landraces, with loose, airy bud structure suited to the humid jungle climate
- **Terpene profile:** Commonly described as intense, psychoactive, and jungle-forward — earthy, incense-like, and occasionally fruity notes are reported
- **Effects:** Associated with a strong, cerebral, and long-lasting psychoactive experience

## Grow Information

- **Climate:** Tropical rainforest; thrives in high humidity and consistent equatorial warmth
- **Flowering time:** 11–15 weeks; patience is required for full expression
- **Height management:** Very tall growth habit; outdoor cultivation in open jungle or agricultural clearings is the traditional approach
- **Mold resistance:** Airy bud structure is generally considered an adaptation to the humid conditions of Aceh's rainforest environment, though growers in high-humidity indoor settings should maintain good airflow
- **Yield:** Grower accounts vary; long flowering and large plant size can produce substantial biomass under optimal equatorial conditions
- **Cultivation notes:** This variety is best suited to experienced growers comfortable with long-season tropical sativas; northern latitude cultivation will require careful light management or greenhouse conditions

## Overview

Phuket is a sativa landrace associated with Thailand's southernmost reaches, growing on and around the island of Phuket and the surrounding coastal tropical lowlands of the Andaman Sea coast. Situated well south of the classic northern Thai highland cannabis regions, this population developed under equatorial-influenced conditions — high year-round humidity, warm temperatures, and pronounced monsoon seasons — giving it a character typically described as a distinctly southern Thai expression rather than a northern highland type.

## History

Cannabis cultivation in southern Thailand has a long, if poorly documented, history tied to local fishing and agricultural communities. Phuket and the surrounding Phang Nga coast were relatively isolated from mainland highland cannabis culture, and the local gene pool is commonly reported to reflect that coastal insularity. Grower accounts suggest the Phuket population circulated among collectors during the 1970s–1980s era of Thai cannabis export, though documentation of this specific southern island variant is sparse compared to better-known northern Thai strains.

## Description

- **Morphology:** Tall, slender sativa structure typical of coastal tropical landraces, with long internodal spacing and narrow leaflets adapted to high humidity.
- **Flowering:** Long-cycling, commonly reported at 11–14 weeks indoors; outdoor timing follows the natural tropical photoperiod.
- **Aroma/Effect:** Grower accounts associate southern Thai sativas with sweet, fruity, and sometimes floral aromas, and an energetic, cerebral effect profile. These characteristics are consistent with lowland coastal sativa populations across the broader region.
- **Climate adaptation:** Suited to coastal tropical conditions — high humidity tolerance, warm temperatures, and strong monsoon resilience.

## Grow Information

- **Height:** Tall; vertical space management is advised for indoor cultivation.
- **Flowering time:** 11–14 weeks; patience is required.
- **Climate:** Performs best in warm, humid, coastal tropical conditions. Growers in temperate climates typically need a long indoor season or a very warm greenhouse to finish.
- **Yields:** Consistent with tall tropical sativas — typically moderate to high given adequate space and light.
- **Notes:** This strain's equatorial-influenced genetics mean it is sensitive to cold and prefers consistently warm nights. Extended vegetative periods are commonly reported when grown indoors under artificial lighting at temperate latitudes.

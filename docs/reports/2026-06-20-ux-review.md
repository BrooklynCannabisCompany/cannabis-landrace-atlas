# UI/UX review — The Cannabis Landrace Atlas

**Date:** 2026-06-20 · **Scope:** the live app (ribbon, map, panel, search, Index, Database, modals/forms, contribution flows). **Status:** review only — no changes made. Suggestions are prioritized; none are blockers.

## Summary

The app is calm, legible, and on-brand: a serif wordmark, a muted "paper" basemap, green leaf markers, and a roomy right-hand reading panel. The information architecture is strong — taxonomy badge + facts, a write-up, exploration cross-links, and a faceted Index. The biggest opportunities are (1) first-run discoverability (a new visitor sees a quiet map with no prompt), (2) mobile/tablet layout (already tracked as #45), and (3) small polish around empty states, loading, and the facet/Index relationship.

## What works well

- **Visual tone** matches the "academic but modern" goal; the panel typography is comfortable for long reading.
- **Taxonomy-first panel** (Morphotype badge, then Chemotype/Domestication/Type with tooltips) communicates a nuanced model clearly, with honest "inferred"/"AI-generated draft" hedging.
- **Exploration links** (Nearby/Regional/Similar) make the dataset feel connected rather than a flat list.
- **Index** with range sliders + one-per-line lists is a genuinely useful second way in.
- **Contribution flows** are low-friction and honest (pre-filled GitHub issues; no dark patterns).

## High-impact

**U1. No first-run guidance / empty state.** On load the user sees a world of small leaves and a search box, with no hint of what to do or how many varieties exist. A new visitor may not realize markers are clickable or that an Index/Database exists.
→ Suggest a lightweight, dismissible intro: a one-line banner or a transient hint ("446 landraces — click a leaf, search, or browse the Index"), or open the About box on first visit (persisted via localStorage). Even placeholder text in the panel area before selection would help.

**U2. Mobile/tablet layout** (tracked as **#45**). At narrow widths the ribbon (wordmark + index button + search + two text buttons) will crowd, and the fixed ~1/3 right panel leaves little map. This is the single most important UX gap for a shareable public link — most visitors will arrive on phones.
→ Bottom-sheet panel, wrapping/condensing ribbon (icons or a single "+" menu for Suggest/Contact), full-width modals. (Already scheduled.)

**U3. Markers overlap badly in dense regions.** Around the Hindu Kush / Central Asia and Southern Africa, leaves pile up and individual varieties are hard to target.
→ Consider marker clustering at low zoom, or slightly smaller markers + spiderfy on click. Even a subtle size reduction with hover-raise would help. (Trade-off: clustering adds a dependency unless hand-rolled.)

## Medium

**M1. "Type (vernacular)" wording is jargon-y for casual users.** The panel leads with "Morphotype: Narrow-Leaf Drug," which is scientifically right but unfamiliar. The tooltip explains it, but tooltips are easy to miss (and absent on touch).
→ Consider a one-glance hint — e.g., show the vernacular in the badge sub-text ("Narrow-Leaf Drug · ~Sativa") or keep the badge but ensure the vernacular row is never below the fold. On touch, tap-to-reveal the definition.

**M2. Facet chips vs. the Index are two different filtering models.** Clicking a panel fact opens a *modal list* of matches; the Index is a *persistent tree*. They don't connect (a facet-list modal can't jump to the same Index node, and vice-versa).
→ Consider unifying: facet chips could open the Index at that facet/value (you already have `openIndex({facet,value})`), giving one consistent "browse by attribute" surface.

**M3. Loading & error states are minimal.** The write-up shows "Loading write-up…" briefly; the map has a single error fallback. There's no skeleton/spinner for the initial 2.5 MB geojson + data fetch on a slow connection.
→ A simple loading indicator on first paint and a friendlier empty-panel state would smooth slow loads.

**M4. Search scope isn't obvious and (currently) excludes AKA.** Placeholder says "strains, regions, countries"; users may try alternate names. (Note: enabling AKA in search is part of the current TLT task.)
→ Once AKA is searchable, reflect it in the placeholder ("search names, aka, regions…").

**M5. The "source" footnote affordance is subtle.** A small superscript "source" after Overview/History is easy to overlook, and only appears when a seed-vendor citation exists.
→ Fine as-is, but a tiny tooltip ("jump to References") on hover/focus would clarify; it already scrolls + flashes, which is nice.

## Low / polish

- **L1.** The Database modal's neocities embed has its own search/scrollbars inside our modal — a nested-scroll feel. Consider a short caption clarifying it's an external companion (the link is already there).
- **L2.** Long Index value-lists (e.g., Sativa = 180+) scroll within the modal with no count-at-a-glance beyond the header number; a sticky facet header would help orientation.
- **L3.** The reset-view (globe) control sits below +/- with no label visible until hover; fine, but a tooltip/title is worth confirming exists.
- **L4.** Empty-slot notes ("use the ⊕ button to suggest one") are good; ensure the ⊕ is large enough to be an obvious tap target (ties into #45).
- **L5.** Consider a subtle hover state on map markers (scale-up) to signal interactivity, complementing the name tooltip.
- **L6.** The panel has no "next/previous variety" or breadcrumb; exploration relies on the related links (which is fine, but a back-stack could help deep browsing).

## Accessibility (recently improved)

Recent work added: modal focus trap + `role=dialog`, search combobox with ↑/↓ + `aria-activedescendant`, tooltip `aria-describedby`, and the Markdown sanitizer. Remaining:
- **A1.** Map markers: confirm keyboard reachability (Leaflet markers are focusable, but the tab order through 446 markers is unwieldy — consider making the map a single landmark and relying on search/Index for keyboard users).
- **A2.** Color-only cues: the selected marker (purple) and green markers differ mainly by color; the shape inversion helps, but verify contrast.
- **A3.** Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for the flyTo animation and the reference-flash.

## Suggested priority

1. **#45 mobile/tablet** (already queued) — biggest reach impact.
2. **U1 first-run guidance** and **M3 loading state** — cheap, high clarity gain.
3. **U3 marker density** — improves the core interaction.
4. **M2 unify facets ↔ Index** — coherence.
5. The rest opportunistically.
